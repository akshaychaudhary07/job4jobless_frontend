Job Alerts

  • Starting Job Alerts
  • Stay ahead in your job search with job4jobless job alerts. Here's how to get started:
    1. Sign In: Log in to your job4jobless account.
    2. Set Up Your Profile: Make sure your profile is complete and up to date, including your preferred job location, industry, and job type.
    3. Search for Jobs: Run a job search with your desired criteria. This is what you want to receive alerts about.
    4. Activate Alerts: After your search results appear, click on the "Set Job Alert" or "Create Job Alert" button, which is usually located near the search bar.
    5. Customize Your Alert: Specify how often you want to receive alerts (daily, weekly), and whether you prefer email or mobile notifications.
    6. Save Alert: Confirm your choices and save the alert. You're all set!
    Now, you'll receive job alerts matching your search criteria directly to your inbox or mobile device, keeping you updated on the latest job opportunities.

  • Stopping Job Alerts
  • In order to stop receiving job alerts, you can easily manage your alerts through your job4jobless account. Here's how:
    1. Log In : Sign in to your job4jobless account.
    2. Access Alerts : Navigate to the "Job Alerts" section in your account settings.
    3. View Alerts : You will see a list of your active job alerts.
    4. Pause or Delete : You have two options:
      • Pause Alert: If you want to temporarily stop receiving alerts, click the "Pause" or "Suspend" button next to the alert you wish to stop temporarily.
      • Delete Alert: To permanently stop receiving alerts, click the "Delete" or "Remove" button next to the alert you want to discontinue.
    5. Confirm : Confirm your choice, and the alert will be paused or deleted.

      6. Follow these steps to easily manage your job alerts, ensuring you receive only the notifications that matter to you.

      Job alerts are a convenient way to stay updated on job openings that match your preferences, and job4jobless makes it easy to start or stop them according to your needs and preferences.

Job Search Tips

  • Getting Started With Your Job Search

    • Job Search Tips can help a jobless person find a relevant job at ease. These tips are highly beneficial and help them look for work that aligns with their requirements and demands.

    Finding work that you can enjoy and that pays you well can be difficult with such an economic crisis. If you are also facing a hard time in searching for your dream job you should follow tips and tricks for job search. Here we are going to discuss some Job Search Tips to help you find work that suits your soul.

  • Tips for Finding jobs
    • Build a solid resume

      • Finding a good job is no longer challenging if you have built a solid resume for yourself. Your resume represents where you have been, what you are looking for, and what you have. It practically speaks for you, represents you, and establishes your first impression. Building a good resume is essential if you want to get your dream job.

    • Internships

      • Internships add value to your resume if you are not experienced. It can be a great way of creating professional connections and kicking off your career well. With some internships, you can learn team management, teamwork, office ethics, speaking skills, and more. Further, it helps eliminate any hesitations and helps you develop solid confidence. That is why it is essential to do hard work and put in your best efforts throughout the internship. Also, you need to learn from your mistakes during your internship period and take on more responsibilities before you get comfortable with your work. It is one of our most prominent job search tips.

    • Follow Company websites

      • If you have a specific niche for jobs, search for different websites that release job vacancies. Regularly looking up the industry can help you understand what is required to get a job in the company. This can boost your confidence and help you reach your goal effortlessly. Another benefit of following company websites is that it will show you what the company is offering and how it functions. Through this, you can determine whether you will be able to fit in or not. Leaving the job after being hired can negatively impact you and staying where you don’t enjoy it is definitely not worth it

    • Keep track of vacancies

      • This is one of the most crucial job search tips. Keeping track of different companies and vacancies can help you look for jobs that suit your niche. When the vacancies are out, you can quickly grab the opportunity and get into an interview. From here, you can get hired depending on the interview. Additionally, you can find crucial information such as the hiring requirements, what the company offers, how it treats the staff, and more.

    • Brief yourself

      • When you are selected for an interview, the first question you will get asked is to brief yourself. To avoid hesitation, develop a brief introduction where you mention everything about yourself. This includes your education, experience, knowledge, hobbies, background, and interests

Types of Job Assessments

Assessment tests for jobs vary depending on industry and company goals. For example, a business that selects employees based on cultural fit may conduct tests to analyse your work style or personality. Here are seven types of job assessments that you could face:

  1. Skills Assessment TestsSkills assessment tests analyse an applicant’s skills. Employers may use situations to test soft skills like critical thinking by asking you to explain how you would approach or resolve them. Many companies hire professionals by skill assessment tests because they are objective and trustworthy.
  2. Job knowledge Tests
    3. Job knowledge tests analyse an applicant’s expertise in a particular field. Employers may use these tests for selecting job positions that require a high level of experience or specialised knowledge. These tests consider your present knowledge rather than your learning capacity.
  3. Personality Tests
    4. Personality tests determine a candidate's interests, preferences, motivation, and interactions. These tests are primarily used to determine whether applicants are culturally compatible with the company or not.
  4. Cognitive Ability Tests
    5. Cognitive ability tests assess a candidate's thought processes, such as reasoning, problem-solving, memory and perception. These tests also include IQ and general aptitude tests. It considers candidates' ability to solve issues or learn new things
  5. Emotional Intelligence Tests
    6. It assesses a candidate's self-awareness, understanding of others, and emotional and relationship management abilities. This is one of the essential tests for jobs that need robust interpersonal skills or leadership
  6. Integrity Tests
    7. Integrity tests to determine a candidate's honesty and trustworthiness. The two types of integrity tests include the Over Integrity Test and the Covert Integrity Test
  7. Physical Ability Test
    8. Physical Ability Test used to analyse applicant’s strength. These tests are essential for those types of jobs that need heavy lifting or being on your feet for the day.
